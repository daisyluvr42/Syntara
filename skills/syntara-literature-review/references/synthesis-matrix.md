# Synthesis Matrix

Build a compact evidence matrix before drafting. Keep it in the conversation unless the user asks for a separate artifact.

Recommended columns:

- Source: cite key, title, year
- Type: primary study, review, guideline, mechanism paper, case series, commentary
- Domain: population, condition, material, technology, or research setting
- Method: design, sample, intervention, measurement
- Finding: the specific result relevant to the review
- Limitation: what weakens or narrows the finding
- Use in review: definition, mechanism, support, contrast, controversy, gap

## Evidence Strength

Use cautious language when:

- evidence is from small samples
- findings are indirect
- sources are reviews rather than original data
- there are conflicting results
- the source only supports a narrower claim than the draft sentence

## Final Checks

Before final output:

- Every strong causal or clinical claim has a citation.
- Review papers are not used as the only support for detailed empirical claims when primary sources are available.
- Each citation is attached to the exact sentence it supports.
- Gaps are stated as gaps in the retrieved evidence, not universal claims about the whole field unless the retrieval is broad enough.
- User notes and Tencent Docs material are treated as structure/style context, not formal literature.
