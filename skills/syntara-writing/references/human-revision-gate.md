# Human Revision Gate

Use this reference in the final style pass when Syntara is polishing, rewriting, or revising user-owned prose. The goal is not to make the text more polished. The goal is to remove friction while keeping the person behind the text present.

## When To Use

Use this gate when:

- the user asks to polish, revise, rewrite, or de-AI a draft;
- a Syntara writing Skill has produced a draft and is running the final style pass;
- the user says the draft lost taste, voice, personality, or "人味儿";
- the task compares an AI draft with the user's edited or final version.

Do not use this gate to rewrite factual source passages, literature evidence, or third-party quotations into the user's voice. Evidence discipline still comes first.

## Core Rule

Change less than feels tempting.

Before changing a sentence, ask whether the original actually blocks reading, weakens evidence, or violates the resolved style profile. If the answer is only "this could sound smoother", keep the original.

## Preserve The Author's Hand

Treat these as possible voice, not defects:

- small redundancies that carry rhythm or hesitation;
- colloquial particles, self-corrections, and unfinished tensions;
- uneven sentence length and paragraph breath;
- plain statements that do not try to sound clever;
- author's own abstraction level, even when a more vivid image is available;
- odd but intentional word choices that fit the writer's position.

Do not replace the user's plain observation with a prettier metaphor, slogan, or balanced sentence unless the original is genuinely unclear.

## Avoid AI Revision Moves

When revising your own generated text or an AI-polished draft, remove or avoid:

- meaning inflation: turning a local fact into a larger era, system, or destiny claim;
- slogan endings such as "future is promising" or "let us wait and see";
- neat contrast formulas such as "not X, but Y" when they are doing fake depth;
- forced three-part structures, parallelism, or short-sentence drama;
- invented scene anchors, details, numbers, or reader emotions;
- marketing words such as "empower", "build", "reshape", "ultimate", "rare", or "luxury" unless source-backed and genre-appropriate;
- replacing repeated nouns with synonyms only to avoid repetition;
- decorative bold, emoji, and chatty handoff phrases.

## Diff-Only Check

For polishing or revision tasks, check only the sentences Syntara changed or added. Do not scan the user's untouched original as if it were AI output. The user's rough edges may be the voice.

If a changed sentence now sounds more generic than the original, restore the original or make a smaller edit.

## Change Ledger

For substantial polish tasks, keep a short change ledger:

1. What changed.
2. Why it needed to change.
3. Whether the change is certain or can be reverted.

For final user-facing output, include the ledger only when the user asked for edit notes or when the revision was sensitive. Otherwise use it internally to keep the pass restrained.

## Revision Learning

When the user edits an AI draft, learn especially from removals:

- what phrases the user cut;
- which polished sentences they made plainer;
- where they restored their own rhythm, hesitation, or directness;
- which AI-like transitions, metaphors, or structures they rejected.

Store durable patterns in the style profile's revision preferences, not as a separate one-off note.

## Final Question

Before handing off, ask internally:

"After my edits, can the reader still feel that this text came from this specific person, in this specific position, paying this specific cost?"

If not, undo the over-polished parts.
